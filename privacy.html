Privacy Policy

Bill Swiper · Last updated: 16 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the independent developer of Bill Swiper (“we”, “us”), the provider of this mobile application (the “App”), collects, uses, and protects information when you use the App. We act as the data controller for the purposes of the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and similar laws.

Bill Swiper works without an account. We do not ask for your name, email, phone number, or any login. We designed the App to collect as little as possible.

1. What we collect

DataWhyStored?
Bill photo / scanned text — the picture of the restaurant bill you take or pick from your gallery, or the text our app reads from it. To recognise the dishes, quantities and prices so you can split the bill. Not stored. Processed in transit to return the result, then discarded.
Anonymous device identifier — a random ID created by Firebase Anonymous Authentication. It is not linked to your identity. To apply the free-usage limit and your Pro status server-side. Yes, while the App is installed (see Retention).
Usage counters — number of scans, Pro entitlement status. To enforce the free tier and unlock Pro. Yes, tied to the anonymous ID.
Purchase data — transaction identifiers and entitlement status for the one-time “Pro” purchase. Payment card details are handled by Apple/Google and never reach us. To grant and restore Pro; fraud prevention. Held by Apple/Google and RevenueCat; we receive status only.
Diagnostics — crash logs and basic, aggregated usage analytics (if enabled in a future version). To fix bugs and improve the App. Aggregated; no bill content.

Stays only on your device

  • Guest names you type are stored locally and never sent to us.
  • If you turn on “Save bill photo to gallery”, the photo is saved to your device only.
  • Your language, currency and sound preferences.

2. What we do NOT collect

  • No name, email, phone or account credentials.
  • No precise location.
  • No contacts, no advertising identifiers (the App shows no ads at this time).
  • We do not sell or “share” your personal information (as defined by the CCPA/CPRA).

4. Legal bases (GDPR/UK GDPR)

  • Performance of a contract — to provide bill recognition and splitting.
  • Legitimate interests — to enforce free-tier limits, prevent abuse, keep the App working and secure.
  • Consent — for optional diagnostics/analytics where required; you can withdraw it any time in settings.

5. International transfers

Our backend runs in the European Union. Some providers (e.g. Google Gemini, RevenueCat) may process data in the United States or other countries. Where required, such transfers rely on the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses and equivalent safeguards.

6. Retention

  • Bill images/text: not retained after processing.
  • Anonymous ID, usage counters, Pro status: kept while you use the App and for a reasonable period afterwards, then deleted or anonymised. Uninstalling the App removes all on-device data.
  • Purchase records: retained by Apple/Google/RevenueCat per their policies and tax/accounting law.

7. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict or object to processing, to data portability, and to withdraw consent (GDPR/UK GDPR). California residents have the right to know, delete, correct, and to opt out of sale/sharing (CCPA/CPRA) — note we do not sell or share personal information. To exercise any right, contact us at kansio.uusi@gmail.com.

Because the App is anonymous, please include the in-app identifier shown in Settings → About → My ID so we can locate the relevant record. You may also lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

8. Children

Bill Swiper is not directed to children and is intended for users aged 16 and over (or the age of digital consent in your country). We do not knowingly collect data from children.

9. Security

Data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS). Backend access is restricted and bill images are processed transiently. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we take reasonable measures to protect your information.

10. Changes

We may update this Policy. We will revise the “Last updated” date and, for material changes, provide notice in the App. Continued use after changes means you accept the updated Policy.
